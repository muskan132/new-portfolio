Published · IEEE Xplore · 2023

A Research Attempt to Predict and Model Personalities through Users’ Social Media Details

Published in IEEE Xplore, where I was the first author. The study asks whether a person's Myers-Briggs personality type can be inferred purely from how they write online. I fine-tuned a pre-trained BERT (large, uncased) transformer on a labelled dataset of users' tweets, framing it as a multi-class problem across all 16 MBTI types and outputting a probability for each type rather than a single hard label. After training across a range of hyperparameter settings and judging performance by F1 score, the model reached a best F1 of 66% with 76% precision.

Published · JUSST Journal · 2022

Analyzing the Correlation between AQI and the Spread of Covid Cases in India

Published in the Journal of University of Shanghai for Science and Technology (JUSST). I analyzed roughly 1.5 years of Covid-19 data across multiple Indian cities — tracking cases, recoveries, and deaths — and processed each city's Air Quality Index over the same window. Aligning lockdown dates against both series surfaced a correlation of around 0.3 between air quality and viral spread, suggesting the approach could generalize to other airborne viruses.

Job Role and Personality Prediction Using CV and Text Analysis

Published in IJRASET. I built a two-sided web application to make hiring faster and fairer. Recruiters can post jobs, set technical screening questions, and review each applicant's results, résumé, a personality report drawn from their social activity, and an overall fit score; candidates can search and apply for roles, answer screening questions, and see the jobs best matched to their profile. By combining CV parsing with text analysis (NLTK / NLP), the system cut recruiter search time by about 75%, improved match accuracy by around 15%, and reduced biased shortlisting by roughly 20%.
